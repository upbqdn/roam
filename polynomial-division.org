#+BEGIN_def
<<.def-nw>> *[[.def-nw][Definition nw]]*. Let
\(h = \sum c_\alpha x^\alpha \in \mathbb{F}[\mathbf{x}] \setminus \{0\}\) be a
nonzero [[file:polynomial.org::.def-bh][polynomial]], \(F\) a subset of
\(\mathbb{F}[\mathbf{x}] \setminus \{0\}\), and let \(\succeq\) be a
[[file:monomial-orders.org::.def-nj][monomial order]] on \(\mathcal{M}\).
1. The *multidegree* of \(h\) is
   \(\mathrm{multideg}(h) = \max(\alpha \in \mathbb{N}_0^n \mid c_\alpha \neq 0)\).
   The maximum is taken with respect to \(\succeq\).
2. The *leading coefficient* of \(h\) is
   \(\mathrm{LC}(h) = c_{\mathrm{multideg}(h)} \in \mathbb{F}\).
3. \(\mathrm{LC}(F) = \{\mathrm{LC}(f) \mid f \in F\}\).
4. The *leading monomial* of \(h\) is
   \(\mathrm{LM}(h) = x^{\mathrm{multideg}(h)}\).
5. \(\mathrm{LM}(F) = \{\mathrm{LM}(f) \mid f \in F\}\).
6. The *leading term* of \(h\) is
   \(\mathrm{LT}(h) = \mathrm{LC}(h) \cdot \mathrm{LM}(h)\).
7. \(\mathrm{LT}(F) = \{\mathrm{LT}(f) \mid f \in F\}\).
8. \(M(f)\) denotes the set of all [[file:polynomial.org::.def-b4][monomials]] in \(f\), and
   \(M(F) = \bigcup_{f \in F} M(f)\).
#+END_def

#+BEGIN_ex
<<.exp-ni>> *[[.exp-ni][Example ni]]*. Let
\(f = xy^2z^3 + xy^3 \in \mathbb{F}[x, y, z]\) be a [[file:polynomial.org::.def-bh][polynomial]] under
the [[file:monomial-orders.org::.def-nk][lexicographic order]]. Then
\(\mathrm{multideg}(f) = (1, 3, 0)\),
\(\mathrm{LC}(f) = 1\),
\(\mathrm{LM}(f) = xy^3\),
\(\mathrm{LT}(f) = xy^3\), and
\(M(f) = \{xy^2z^3, xy^3\}\).
#+END_ex

Univariate division provides an introduction to multivariate division and is
used in the construction of [[file:field.org::.rem-bz][finite fields]].

#+BEGIN_pr
<<.thm-ns>> *[[.thm-ns][Theorem ns]]*. (/Univariate Division/.) Let
\(g(x) \in \mathbb{F}[x]\) be a univariate [[file:polynomial.org::.def-bh][polynomial]]. Then every
\(f(x) \in \mathbb{F}[x]\) can be written as
\(f(x) = q(x) g(x) + r(x)\), where \(q(x), r(x) \in \mathbb{F}[x]\) are unique
and either \(r(x) = 0\) or \(\deg(r(x)) < \deg(g(x))\) [cite:@iva].
#+END_pr

We can use the following algorithm for finding the [[file:polynomial.org::.def-bh][polynomials]] \(q\)
and \(r\).

#+begin_center
<<.alg-d1>> *[[.alg-d1][Algorithm d1]]*. Univariate polynomial division.
#+end_center

*Input:* univariate polynomials \(g, f\).\\
*Output:* univariate polynomials \(q, r\).
1. \(q \gets 0\), \(r \gets f\).
2. *while* \(r \neq 0\) and \(\mathrm{LT}(g)\) divides \(\mathrm{LT}(r)\):
   - \(q \gets q + \mathrm{LT}(r) / \mathrm{LT}(g)\).
   - \(r \gets r - (\mathrm{LT}(r) / \mathrm{LT}(g)) \cdot g\).
3. *return* \(q, r\).

#+BEGIN_ex
<<.exp-du>> *[[.exp-du][Example du]]*. Let \(f(x) \in \mathbb{F}[x]\) be a
univariate [[file:polynomial.org::.def-bh][polynomial]] of degree
\(d = \deg(f(x))\) and \(\langle f(x) \rangle\) the [[file:ideal.org::.def-bs][ideal]]
generated by \(f(x)\). The elements of the [[file:ideal.org::.rem-bi][quotient ring]]
\(\mathbb{F}[x] / \langle f(x) \rangle\) can be written as univariate
polynomials \(c_{d-1} x^{d-1} + \ldots + c_1 x + c_0\) in \(\mathbb{F}[x]\) of
degree less than \(d\). In this quotient ring, we use standard addition.
However, we define multiplication by applying [[.thm-ns][Theorem ns]]: given
two univariate polynomials \(g(x), h(x)\), there exist \(q(x), r(x)\) such that
\(g(x) h(x) = q(x) f(x) + r(x)\), where \(\deg(r(x)) < \deg(f(x)) = d\). We
take \(r(x)\) as the product of \(g(x)\) and \(h(x)\).
#+END_ex

#+BEGIN_pr
<<.prp-nz>> *[[.prp-nz][Proposition nz]]*. Let \(\mathbb{F}\) be a [[file:field.org::.rem-bz][finite field]]
of order \(q = p^n\) and \(f(x) \in \mathbb{F}[x]\) a univariate
[[file:polynomial.org::.def-bh][polynomial]] of degree \(d\). The elements of the [[file:ideal.org::.rem-bi][quotient ring]]
\(\mathbb{F}[x] / \langle f(x) \rangle\) can be written as polynomials of
degree less than \(d\), with multiplication defined via the remainder on
division by \(f(x)\). This quotient ring is a finite field of order
\(q^d = p^{nd}\) if and only if \(f(x)\) is
[[file:polynomial.org::.def-ny][irreducible]] over \(\mathbb{F}[x]\) [cite:@lidl].
#+END_pr

#+BEGIN_pr
<<.prp-na>> *[[.prp-na][Proposition na]]*. Let
\(f \in \mathbb{F}[\mathbf{x}]\) be [[file:polynomial.org::.def-ny][irreducible]] over the
[[file:field.org::.def-8][field]] \(\mathbb{F}\). Then there exists an [[file:field.org::.def-b3][extension]] of
\(\mathbb{F}\) containing a [[file:affine-variety.org::.rem-nn][root]] of \(f\).

/Proof./ Consider the [[file:field.org::.rem-bz][finite field]]
\(\mathbb{E} = \mathbb{F}[\mathbf{x}] / \langle f \rangle\). The elements of
\(\mathbb{E}\) are the [[file:ideal.org::.def-bw][cosets]]
\([r] = r + \langle f \rangle\) with
\(r \in \mathbb{F}[\mathbf{x}]\). For any \(a \in \mathbb{F}\), we can obtain a
coset \([a]\) determined by the constant [[file:polynomial.org::.def-bh][polynomial]] \(a\). The mapping
\(a \mapsto [a]\) gives an isomorphism from \(\mathbb{F}\) onto a subfield
\(\mathbb{F}'\) of \(\mathbb{E}\), so that \(\mathbb{F}'\) may be identified
with \(\mathbb{F}\). Particularly, \(\mathbb{E}\) can be seen as an
[[file:field.org::.def-b3][extension]] of \(\mathbb{F}\). Now, for every
\(r(x) = a_0 + a_1 x + \cdots + a_m x^m \in \mathbb{F}[\mathbf{x}]\) we have
\([r] = [a_0] + [a_1][x] + \cdots + [a_m][x]^m = a_0 + a_1[x] + \cdots + a_m[x]^m\)
by the rules for operating with cosets and the identification \([a_i] = a_i\).
Hence, every element of \(\mathbb{E}\) can be written as a polynomial expression
in \([x]\) with coefficients in \(\mathbb{F}\). We say that the [[file:field.org::.def-b3][extension field]]
\(\mathbb{E}\) is obtained by *adjoining* \([x]\) and denote it
\(\mathbb{F}([x])\). Note that if
\(f(x) = b_0 + b_1 x + \cdots + b_n x^n\), then
\(f([x]) = b_0 + b_1[x] + \cdots + b_n[x]^n = [f] = [0]\), so that \([x]\) is
a root of \(f\).
#+END_pr

We use multivariate division in the description of algorithms for computing
[[id:groebner-basis][Gr√∂bner bases]].

#+BEGIN_pr
<<.thm-n3>> *[[.thm-n3][Theorem n3]]*. (/Multivariate Division/.) Let \(\succeq\) be a
[[file:monomial-orders.org::.def-nj][monomial order]] on \(\mathcal{M}\) and let
\(F = (f_1, \ldots, f_m)\) be an ordered \(m\)-tuple of [[file:polynomial.org::.def-bh][polynomials]] in
\(\mathbb{F}[\mathbf{x}]\). Then every
\(f \in \mathbb{F}[\mathbf{x}]\) can be written as
\[f = q_1 f_1 + \cdots + q_m f_m + r,\]
where \(q_i, r \in \mathbb{F}[\mathbf{x}]\), and either \(r = 0\) or \(r\) is a
linear combination, with coefficients in \(\mathbb{F}\), of
[[file:polynomial.org::.def-b4][monomials]], none of which is divisible by any of
\(\mathrm{LT}(f_1), \ldots, \mathrm{LT}(f_m)\). We call \(r\) a *remainder* of
\(f\) on division by \(F\) and denote it \(\overline{f}^F\). Furthermore, if
\(q_i f_i \neq 0\), then
\(\mathrm{LM}(f) \succeq \mathrm{LM}(q_i f_i)\) [cite:@iva].
#+END_pr

We can use the following algorithm for finding the [[file:polynomial.org::.def-bh][polynomials]]
\(q_i\) and \(r\).

#+begin_center
<<.alg-dw>> *[[.alg-dw][Algorithm dw]]*. Multivariate polynomial division.
#+end_center

*Input:* polynomials \(f_1, \ldots, f_m, f\).\\
*Output:* polynomials \(q_1, \ldots, q_m, r\).
1. \(q_i \gets 0\) for all \(i\), \(r \gets 0\), \(p \gets f\).
2. *while* \(p \neq 0\):
   - \(i \gets 1\), \(\mathit{divisionoccured} \gets \mathit{False}\).
   - *while* \(i \le m\) and \(\mathit{divisionoccured} = \mathit{False}\):
     - *if* \(\mathrm{LT}(f_i)\) divides \(\mathrm{LT}(p)\):
       - \(q_i \gets q_i + \mathrm{LT}(p) / \mathrm{LT}(f_i)\).
       - \(p \gets p - (\mathrm{LT}(p) / \mathrm{LT}(f_i)) \cdot f_i\).
       - \(\mathit{divisionoccured} \gets \mathit{True}\).
     - *else* \(i \gets i + 1\).
   - *if* \(\mathit{divisionoccured} = \mathit{False}\):
     - \(r \gets r + \mathrm{LT}(p)\), \(p \gets p - \mathrm{LT}(p)\).
3. *return* \(q_1, \ldots, q_m, r\).
